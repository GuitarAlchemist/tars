## ❌ Why LLM-Only Paths Plateau

LLMs are:
- ✅ Phenomenal at pattern completion
- ❌ Terrible at persistent identity
- ❌ Weak at long-horizon planning
- ❌ Unsafe for self-modification
- ❌ Amnesic across runs

Even frontier models fail at:
- Tracking commitments
- Remembering *why* a belief exists
- Explaining *why* a plan changed
- Accumulating durable knowledge

**Scaling helps, but the failure modes stay.**

---

## ❌ Why Classical Symbolic AI Failed

Old symbolic systems:
- Required humans to author all knowledge
- Were brittle
- Couldn't generalize
- Couldn't learn from noise
- Collapsed under combinatorics

**They had structure but no imagination.**

**None of those emerge from gradients alone.**

## 📐 Design Principles

### 1. Symbols Are Earned, Not Assumed

Beliefs enter the ledger only through:
- Evidence (external sources with provenance)
- Inference (from existing beliefs, logged)
- LLM proposals (verified before acceptance)

### 2. LLMs Are Subordinate, Not Sovereign

LLMs may:
- Propose beliefs
- Generate plans
- Summarize knowledge

LLMs may NOT:
- Write directly to the ledger
- Delete beliefs without evidence
- Override symbolic constraints

### 3. Plans Are Forked, Not Overwritten

When evidence invalidates a plan:
- Old plan is marked `superseded`
- New plan is created with explicit `supersedes` link
- Both remain in the ledger

### 4. Evolution Is Logged, Not Forgotten

Every change records:
- What changed
- Why it changed
- What evidence triggered it
- What the previous state was
